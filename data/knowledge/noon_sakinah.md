# Rules of Noon Sakinah (نون ساكنة) and Tanween (تنوين)

rule_name: Noon Sakinah and Tanween

## Definition
Noon Sakinah (نون ساكنة) is a noon (ن) with a sukoon (ـْ) that appears in the middle or at the end of a word.
Tanween (تنوين) is a double vowel (ً ٍ ٌ) that appears only at the end of nouns and adjectives. It is treated exactly like Noon Sakinah in all four rules.

There are four rules that apply when Noon Sakinah or Tanween is followed by another letter:

---

## Rule 1: Izhar (إظهار) — Clear Pronunciation

rule_name: Izhar

### Definition
Izhar (إظهار) means "to make clear." The noon sakinah or tanween is pronounced clearly without any merging, hiding, or nasalization (ghunna).

### Letters (6 letters — حروف الحلق)
These are the six throat letters (huroof al-halq):
ء (hamza) — ه (ha) — ع (ain) — ح (ha) — غ (ghain) — خ (kha)

Memory aid: أَخِي هَاكَ عِلْمًا حَازَهُ غَيْرُ خَاسِرٍ

### How to Recite
When noon sakinah or tanween is followed by any of the six throat letters, pronounce the noon fully and clearly, then move to the next letter. There is no ghunna (nasalization) extended.

### Quran Examples
- مَنْ آمَنَ (man aamana) — Surah Al-Baqarah 2:62 — noon sakinah before hamza (ء)
- مِنْ هَادٍ (min haadin) — Surah Ar-Ra'd 13:33 — noon sakinah before ha (ه)
- مَنْ عَمِلَ (man 'amila) — Surah Az-Zalzalah 99:7 — noon sakinah before ain (ع)
- أَنْحَاءَ — noon sakinah before ha (ح)
- مِنْ غِلٍّ (min ghillin) — Surah Al-A'raf 7:43 — noon sakinah before ghain (غ)
- وَمَنْ خَفَّتْ (wa man khaffat) — Surah Al-A'raf 7:9 — noon sakinah before kha (خ)
- عَلِيمٌ حَكِيمٌ (aleemun hakeemun) — tanween before ha (ح)

---

## Rule 2: Idgham (إدغام) — Merging

rule_name: Idgham

### Definition
Idgham (إدغام) means "to merge." The noon sakinah or tanween merges into the following letter so that the two become one stressed (mushaddad) letter.

Idgham only occurs when the noon sakinah is at the end of a word and the following letter begins the next word. It does NOT apply within a single word (e.g., دُنْيَا, قِنْوَانٌ, بُنْيَانٌ — these are read with Izhar because noon and the letter are in the same word).

### Letters (6 letters): ي ر م ل و ن
Memory aid: يَرْمَلُونَ (yarmaloon)

Idgham is divided into two types:

---

### 2a. Idgham with Ghunna (إدغام بغنة)

rule_name: Idgham with Ghunna

#### Letters (4 letters): ي ن م و
Memory aid: يَنْمُو (yanmoo — it grows)

#### How to Recite
Merge the noon sakinah or tanween completely into the following letter while maintaining a nasalization (ghunna) of 2 counts (harakatayn).

#### Quran Examples
- مَن يَعْمَلْ (man ya'mal) — Surah Az-Zalzalah 99:7 — noon merges into ya (ي)
- مِن نِعْمَةٍ (min ni'matin) — Surah An-Nahl 16:53 — noon merges into noon (ن)
- مِن مَاءٍ (min maa'in) — Surah Al-Anbiya 21:30 — noon merges into meem (م)
- مِن وَلِيٍّ (min waliyyin) — Surah Al-Kahf 18:17 — noon merges into waw (و)
- يَوْمَئِذٍ يَصْدُرُ (yawma'idhin yasduru) — Surah Az-Zalzalah 99:6 — tanween before ya (ي)
- رِيحًا صَرْصَرًا وَنُحُوسٍ — tanween before waw (و)

---

### 2b. Idgham without Ghunna (إدغام بلا غنة)

rule_name: Idgham without Ghunna

#### Letters (2 letters): ل ر

#### How to Recite
Merge the noon sakinah or tanween completely into the following letter (lam or ra) WITHOUT any ghunna. The noon disappears entirely into the next letter.

#### Quran Examples
- مِن رَبِّهِمْ (min rabbihim) — Surah Al-Baqarah 2:5 — noon merges into ra (ر)
- مِن لَدُنْهُ (min ladunhu) — Surah Al-Kahf 18:2 — noon merges into lam (ل)
- هُدًى لِلْمُتَّقِينَ (hudan lil-muttaqeen) — Surah Al-Baqarah 2:2 — tanween merges into lam (ل)
- غَفُورٌ رَحِيمٌ (ghafoorun raheemun) — common ending — tanween merges into ra (ر)

---

## Rule 3: Iqlab (إقلاب) — Conversion

rule_name: Iqlab

### Definition
Iqlab (إقلاب) means "to convert" or "to change." The noon sakinah or tanween is converted into a meem (م) sound, then that meem is hidden (ikhfa) before the letter ba (ب), with ghunna of 2 counts.

### Letter (1 letter only): ب (ba)

### How to Recite
1. Convert the noon sakinah or tanween into a meem (م)
2. Close the lips (as if saying meem)
3. Maintain a ghunna (nasalization) of 2 counts
4. Do NOT separate the lips until you pronounce the ba (ب)

In Quran mushaf, Iqlab is marked with a small meem (م) above the noon or tanween.

### Quran Examples
- مِن بَعْدِ (min ba'di) — Surah Al-Baqarah 2:27 — noon becomes meem before ba (ب)
- أَنبِئْهُم (anbi'hum) — Surah Al-Baqarah 2:33 — noon before ba within word
- سَمِيعٌ بَصِيرٌ (samee'un baseerun) — Surah Al-Isra 17:1 — tanween before ba (ب)
- عَلِيمٌ بِذَاتِ الصُّدُورِ — tanween before ba (ب)

---

## Rule 4: Ikhfa (إخفاء) — Concealment

rule_name: Ikhfa

### Definition
Ikhfa (إخفاء) means "to conceal" or "to hide." The noon sakinah or tanween is pronounced in a state between Izhar (clear) and Idgham (merged), with ghunna (nasalization) held for 2 counts. The tongue does NOT fully touch the articulation point of the noon, and does NOT fully merge into the next letter.

### Letters (15 letters)
All remaining letters of the Arabic alphabet that are not in Izhar, Idgham, or Iqlab:
ت ث ج د ذ ز س ش ص ض ط ظ ف ق ك

Memory poem:
صِفْ ذَا ثَنَا كَمْ جَادَ شَخْصٌ قَدْ سَمَا
دُمْ طَيِّبًا زِدْ فِي تُقًى ضَعْ ظَالِمًا

### How to Recite
- The tongue tip approaches (but does not touch) the articulation point of the noon
- Maintain a ghunna of 2 counts
- The strength of the concealment varies by letter (stronger for ت د ط ظ ذ, lighter for ف ق ك)

### Quran Examples
- مِن تَحْتِهَا (min tahtiha) — Surah Al-Baqarah 2:25 — noon before ta (ت)
- مَن ثَقُلَتْ (man thaqulat) — Surah Al-A'raf 7:8 — noon before tha (ث)
- مِنْ جُوعٍ (min joo'in) — Surah An-Nahl 16:112 — noon before jeem (ج)
- مِن دُونِهِ (min doonihi) — Surah Al-Kahf 18:15 — noon before dal (د)
- مِن ذَهَبٍ (min dhahabin) — Surah Al-Kahf 18:31 — noon before dhal (ذ)
- مِن زَوْجَيْنِ (min zawjayni) — Surah Az-Zumar 39:6 — noon before zayn (ز)
- مِن سَبَإٍ (min Saba'in) — Surah An-Naml 27:22 — noon before seen (س)
- مِن شَيْءٍ (min shay'in) — Surah Yunus 10:61 — noon before sheen (ش)
- أَنصَارَ اللهِ (ansaarallah) — Surah As-Saff 61:14 — noon before sad (ص)
- مَن ضَلَّ (man dalla) — Surah Al-A'raf 7:178 — noon before dad (ض)
- كُنتُمْ (kuntum) — Surah Al-Baqarah 2:23 — noon before ta (ط)
- مِن ظَهِيرٍ (min dhaheer) — Surah Al-Isra 17:88 — noon before dha (ظ)
- أَنفُسَكُمْ (anfusakum) — Surah Al-Baqarah 2:54 — noon before fa (ف)
- مِن قَبْلِ (min qabli) — Surah Al-Baqarah 2:25 — noon before qaf (ق)
- مَن كَفَرَ (man kafara) — Surah Al-Imran 3:177 — noon before kaf (ك)
- خَيْرًا كَثِيرًا (khayran katheeran) — Surah Al-Baqarah 2:269 — tanween before kaf (ك)
