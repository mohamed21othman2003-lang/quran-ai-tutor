# Rules of Meem Sakinah (ميم ساكنة)

rule_name: Meem Sakinah

## Definition
Meem Sakinah (ميم ساكنة) is a meem (م) carrying a sukoon (ـْ), appearing in the middle or at the end of a word. When meem sakinah is followed by another letter, one of three rules applies.

Unlike Noon Sakinah which has four rules, Meem Sakinah has only three rules — and they depend entirely on the letter that follows the meem.

---

## Rule 1: Ikhfa Shafawi (إخفاء شفوي) — Labial Concealment

rule_name: Ikhfa Shafawi

### Definition
Ikhfa Shafawi (إخفاء شفوي) means "labial concealment." The word shafawi (شفوي) comes from shafa (شفة) meaning "lip," because both the meem and the ba are labial (lip) letters.

When meem sakinah is followed by the letter ba (ب), the meem is concealed — pronounced between a clear meem and a fully merged meem — while the lips remain lightly closed (not pressed together as in a full meem) and a ghunna (nasalization) is maintained for 2 counts.

### Letter: ب (ba) only

### How to Recite
1. When you reach the meem sakinah before a ba, partially close the lips
2. Maintain a ghunna (nasal sound from the nose) for 2 counts (harakatayn)
3. Do not fully press the lips together as you would for a clear meem
4. Then release into the ba

The key distinction: lips are gently closed with air passing through the nose — not a full closure as in Izhar Shafawi.

### Quran Examples
- وَمَا هُم بِمُؤْمِنِينَ (wa maa hum bimu'mineen) — Surah Al-Baqarah 2:8 — meem sakinah before ba (ب)
- يَعْتَصِم بِاللهِ (ya'tasim billah) — Surah Al-Imran 3:101 — meem sakinah before ba (ب)
- تَرْمِيهِم بِحِجَارَةٍ (tarmeehim bihijaarah) — Surah Al-Feel 105:4 — meem sakinah before ba (ب)
- رَبَّهُم بِالْغَيْبِ (rabbahum bil-ghayb) — Surah Al-Anbiya — meem sakinah before ba (ب)

## Rule 2: Idgham Shafawi (إدغام شفوي) — Labial Merging

rule_name: Idgham Shafawi

### Definition
Idgham Shafawi (إدغام شفوي) means "labial merging." When meem sakinah is followed by another meem (م), the first meem (sakinah) merges completely into the second meem, which is then pronounced as one stressed (mushaddad) meem with a ghunna of 2 counts.

### Letter: م (meem) only

### How to Recite
1. When you reach the meem sakinah followed by another meem
2. Merge the two meems into one stressed meem (مّ)
3. Maintain a ghunna (nasalization) of 2 counts
4. The first meem disappears completely into the second

This is also called Idgham Mithlayn Sagheer (إدغام المثلين الصغير) — merging of two identical letters, the first being sakin and the second being mutaharrik (with a vowel).

### Quran Examples
- كَم مِن فِئَةٍ (kam min fi'atin) — Surah Al-Baqarah 2:249 — meem merges into meem
- وَهُم مِن بَعْدِ (wa hum min ba'di) — meem sakinah merges into meem
- لَهُم مَا يَشَاءُونَ (lahum maa yashaaoona) — Surah Ya-Seen 36:57 — meem sakinah before meem
- أَمَمًا مِنكُم — meem sakinah before meem
- فِيهِم مَن (feehim man) — meem sakinah merges into following meem

## Rule 3: Izhar Shafawi (إظهار شفوي) — Labial Clarity

rule_name: Izhar Shafawi

### Definition
Izhar Shafawi (إظهار شفوي) means "labial clarity." When meem sakinah is followed by any letter other than ba (ب) or meem (م), the meem must be pronounced clearly and completely, without any merging, concealment, or nasalization (ghunna).

### Letters: All 26 remaining Arabic letters (everything except ب and م)

The most commonly tested letters are:
- الواو (و) — wa — because meem and waw are both labial letters, students may accidentally merge them; they must be kept clear
- الفاء (ف) — fa — also labial; must not be merged with meem

### How to Recite
1. Pronounce the meem sakinah fully and clearly
2. Press the lips together completely for the meem
3. Release cleanly into the next letter
4. No ghunna beyond the natural resonance of the meem

Special attention: When meem sakinah is before waw (و) or fa (ف), extra care is needed to avoid unintentional merging because these are also labial sounds. Scholars specifically highlight these two letters.

### Quran Examples
- أَنعَمتَ عَلَيهِم غَيرِ (an'amta 'alayhim ghayri) — Surah Al-Fatiha 1:7 — meem sakinah before ghain (غ)
- وَلَهُم عَذَابٌ (wa lahum 'adhabun) — Surah Al-Baqarah 2:7 — meem sakinah before ain (ع)
- لَهُم فِيهَا (lahum feehaa) — Surah Al-Baqarah 2:25 — meem sakinah before fa (ف) — must be clear
- هُم وَأَزْوَاجُهُمْ (hum wa azwaajuhum) — Surah Ya-Seen 36:56 — meem sakinah before waw (و) — must be clear
- عَلَيهِم وَلَا (alayhim walaa) — Surah Al-Fatiha 1:7 — meem sakinah before waw (و)
- أَصحَابُ الجَنَّةِ هُم فِيهَا — meem sakinah before fa (ف)
- لَهُم سُوءُ الحِسَابِ — meem sakinah before seen (س)
