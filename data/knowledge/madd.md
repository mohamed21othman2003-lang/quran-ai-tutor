# Madd Rules (أحكام المد)

rule_name: Madd

## Definition
Madd (مد) means "elongation" or "stretching." In Tajweed, Madd refers to the lengthening of certain vowel sounds beyond their natural duration. The unit of measurement is the haraka (حركة) — the time it takes to pronounce one short vowel.

### The Three Madd Letters (حروف المد)
Madd occurs on three letters when preceded by the matching short vowel:
1. أَلِف (ا) — preceded by a fatha (فتحة) — produces the "aa" sound
2. وَاو (و) with sukoon — preceded by a damma (ضمة) — produces the "oo" sound
3. يَاء (ي) with sukoon — preceded by a kasra (كسرة) — produces the "ee" sound

Madd is divided into two main categories: Asli (original/natural) and Far'i (secondary/extended).

---

## Part 1: Madd Asli / Tabee'i (مد أصلي / طبيعي) — Natural Madd

rule_name: Madd Tabee'i

### Definition
Madd Tabee'i (مد طبيعي) means "natural elongation." It is the foundation of all Madd rules. The madd letter is not followed by a hamza (ء) or a sukoon (ـْ) in any recitation scenario.

### Duration: 2 counts (harakatayn)

### Conditions
- A madd letter (ا / و / ي) is present
- The letter before it carries the matching short vowel
- No hamza or sukoon follows immediately after the madd letter in the same word or in any pause/continuation scenario

### Quran Examples
- قَالَ (qaala) — Surah Al-Baqarah — the alif is 2 counts
- يَقُولُ (yaqoolu) — the waw is 2 counts
- قِيلَ (qeela) — the ya is 2 counts
- الرَّحْمَـٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ (ar-rahmaani ar-raheem) — Surah Al-Fatiha 1:3 — raheem: ya is 2 counts
- نَسْتَعِينُ (nasta'eenu) — Surah Al-Fatiha 1:5 — ya and waw are 2 counts each
- كِتَابٌ (kitaabun) — alif is 2 counts

### Special Cases of Madd Tabee'i
- **Madd Badal**: A hamza precedes the madd letter (e.g., آمَنَ — originally أَأْمَنَ), duration 2 counts in Hafs recitation
- **Madd 'Iwad**: A tanween fathatain (ـًـ) paused upon becomes an alif sound, 2 counts (e.g., pausing on عَلِيمًا → عَلِيمَا)
- **Madd Tamkeen**: Two ya letters meet, first with kasra and shadda, second with sukoon (e.g., النَّبِيِّينَ)

---

## Part 2: Madd Far'i (مد فرعي) — Secondary Madd

Madd Far'i is caused by either a hamza (ء) or a sukoon (ـْ) occurring after the madd letter. It is longer than Madd Tabee'i.

---

### 2a. Madd Muttasil (مد متصل) — Connected Madd

rule_name: Madd Muttasil

#### Definition
Madd Muttasil (مد متصل) means "connected elongation." The madd letter and the hamza (ء) that follows it are in the SAME word. This is obligatory (واجب) — all reciters must extend it.

#### Duration: 4 to 5 counts (harakaat)
- In Hafs 'an 'Asim: 4 or 5 counts (both are acceptable; most common recitation is 4-5)

#### Condition
Madd letter followed immediately by hamza within the same word.

#### Quran Examples
- جَاءَ (jaa'a) — Surah Al-Baqarah 2:87 — alif madd then hamza, same word
- شَاءَ (shaa'a) — alif then hamza, same word — 4-5 counts
- سَوَاءٌ (sawaa'un) — Surah Al-Baqarah 2:6 — waw madd then hamza
- جِيءَ (jee'a) — ya madd then hamza, same word
- السَّمَاءِ (as-samaa'i) — Surah Al-Baqarah 2:22 — alif then hamza
- يَشَاءُ (yashaa'u) — Surah Al-Baqarah 2:90 — alif then hamza
- السُّوءِ (as-soo'i) — waw madd then hamza
- يُضِيءُ (yudee'u) — ya madd then hamza

---

### 2b. Madd Munfasil (مد منفصل) — Separated Madd

rule_name: Madd Munfasil

#### Definition
Madd Munfasil (مد منفصل) means "separated elongation." The madd letter is at the END of one word and the hamza (ء) begins the NEXT word. This is permitted (جائز) — reciters may choose from 2 to 5 counts.

#### Duration: 2 to 5 counts
- In Hafs 'an 'Asim (common recitation): 4 or 5 counts (preferred)
- Minimum: 2 counts (like natural madd)

#### Condition
Madd letter at end of word, followed by a word beginning with hamza, recited in continuation (wasl).

#### Quran Examples
- إِنَّا أَعْطَيْنَاكَ (innaa a'taynaka) — Surah Al-Kawthar 108:1 — alif then hamza of next word
- يَا أَيُّهَا (yaa ayyuha) — Surah Al-Hujurat 49:13 — alif then hamza
- قُوا أَنفُسَكُمْ (qoo anfusakum) — Surah At-Tahrim 66:6 — waw then hamza
- بِمَا أُنزِلَ (bimaa unzila) — Surah Al-Baqarah 2:4 — alif then hamza
- فِي أَنفُسِكُمْ (fee anfusikum) — ya then hamza of next word
- الَّذِي أَنقَضَ (alladhee anqadha) — Surah Al-Inshirah 94:3 — ya then hamza

---

### 2c. Madd Lazim (مد لازم) — Compulsory Madd

rule_name: Madd Lazim

#### Definition
Madd Lazim (مد لازم) means "compulsory elongation." It is the longest madd and is mandatory for ALL reciters. It occurs when a madd letter is followed by a letter with a permanent sukoon (ـْ) — either within a word or within a letter.

#### Duration: 6 counts (always, no variation)

#### Types of Madd Lazim

**a) Kalimi Muthaqqal (كلمي مثقل)** — Word Madd, with shaddah
- The sukoon is the first half of a shaddah (double letter)
- Example: دَابَّة (daabbah) — Surah Al-Baqarah 2:164 — alif before ba with shaddah
- Example: الحَاقَّةُ (al-haaqqah) — Surah Al-Haqqah 69:1

**b) Kalimi Mukhaffaf (كلمي مخفف)** — Word Madd, without shaddah
- The sukoon is a true resting sukoon, not a shaddah
- Very rare in the Quran
- Example: آلْآنَ (aal-aana) — Surah Yunus 10:51, 91 — occurs only twice

**c) Harfi Muthaqqal (حرفي مثقل)** — Letter Madd, with shaddah
- Occurs in the disconnected letters (الحروف المقطعة) at the beginning of surahs
- The letter when spelled out contains a madd followed by a shaddah
- Examples: لام (lam) in الم — alif then lam with shaddah → 6 counts
- الم (Alif Lam Meem) — Surah Al-Baqarah 2:1 — each letter: ا (2), لام (6), ميم (6)
- المص — Surah Al-A'raf 7:1
- الر — ra in الر is 6 counts
- طسم — Surah Ash-Shu'ara 26:1

**d) Harfi Mukhaffaf (حرفي مخفف)** — Letter Madd, without shaddah
- Disconnected letters spelled with madd but without shaddah following
- Examples: حم (Ha-Meem) — Surah Ghafir 40:1 — ha is 2 counts, meem is 6 counts
- يس (Ya-Seen) — Surah Ya-Seen 36:1 — ya is 2 counts, seen is 6 counts

#### Key Quran Examples for Madd Lazim
- الم (Alif Lam Meem) — Surah Al-Baqarah 2:1, Surah Al-Imran 3:1
- الر (Alif Lam Ra) — Surah Yunus 10:1, Surah Yusuf 12:1
- طسم (Ta Sin Meem) — Surah Ash-Shu'ara 26:1
- يس (Ya Seen) — Surah Ya-Seen 36:1
- ص (Sad) — Surah Sad 38:1
- ق (Qaf) — Surah Qaf 50:1
- ن (Nun) — Surah Al-Qalam 68:1
- دَابَّةٌ (daabbatun) — within a word, alif before stressed ba

## Summary Table

| Madd Type | Cause | Duration | Obligation |
|-----------|-------|----------|------------|
| Tabee'i (طبيعي) | No hamza or sukoon follows | 2 counts | Mandatory |
| Muttasil (متصل) | Hamza in same word | 4–5 counts | Mandatory (Wajib) |
| Munfasil (منفصل) | Hamza in next word | 2–5 counts | Permitted (Ja'iz) |
| Lazim (لازم) | Permanent sukoon follows | 6 counts | Mandatory |
