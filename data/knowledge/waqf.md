# Waqf Rules (أحكام الوقف)

rule_name: Waqf

## Definition
Waqf (وقف) means "stopping" or "pausing" in recitation. It refers to interrupting the recitation at the end of a word while taking a breath, before resuming from an appropriate point. Waqf is essential knowledge in Tajweed because stopping at the wrong place can distort the meaning of the Quran.

The opposite of Waqf is Wasl (وصل) — continuing the recitation without stopping.

Ibtida' (ابتداء) refers to where you resume after the pause. A proper Waqf must be followed by a proper Ibtida'.

---

## Waqf Symbols in the Mushaf

Modern printed Qurans (especially the Medina Mushaf and Hafs recitation copies) use standardized symbols to guide the reciter on where to stop, where to continue, and where stopping is preferred or discouraged.

---

### Symbol: م (Waqf Lazim — وقف لازم)

rule_name: Waqf Lazim

#### Name: Compulsory Stop (الوقف اللازم) / Waqf Manzil
#### Symbol: مـ (the letter meem, from لازم)

#### Definition
Waqf Lazim means stopping is COMPULSORY (obligatory). If the reciter does not stop here, the meaning of the verse will be corrupted or reversed. This is the most critical waqf symbol in the Quran.

#### How to Recite
- You MUST stop here
- Take a breath if needed
- Resume from the beginning of the next phrase (or from the waqf point itself if it is a complete phrase)

#### Quran Examples
- Surah Al-Baqarah 2:2: ذَٰلِكَ الْكِتَابُ لَا رَيْبَ **مـ** فِيهِ
  → Must stop on لَا رَيْبَ — "there is no doubt [in it]"
  → If you continue without stopping: لَا رَيْبَ فِيهِ هُدًى — the sentence fragments
  → Stopping here makes clear: "no doubt" — then "in it is guidance"
- Surah Al-An'am 6:36: إِنَّمَا يَسْتَجِيبُ الَّذِينَ يَسْمَعُونَ **مـ** وَالْمَوْتَىٰ

---

### Symbol: ط (Waqf Mutlaq — وقف مطلق)

rule_name: Waqf Mutlaq

#### Name: Absolute Stop (الوقف المطلق)
#### Symbol: ط (the letter ta, from مُطلَق)

#### Definition
Waqf Mutlaq means stopping is PREFERRED. The meaning is complete at this point, making it the best place to pause. The sentence before the symbol is grammatically and semantically complete.

#### How to Recite
- Stopping here is preferred (mustahabb)
- Continuing (wasl) is also acceptable but stopping is better
- After stopping, resume from the next word

#### Quran Examples
- Surah Al-Fatiha 1:4: مَالِكِ يَوْمِ الدِّينِ **ط**
  → The phrase "Master of the Day of Judgment" is complete — preferred stop
- Surah Al-Baqarah 2:5: أُولَٰئِكَ عَلَىٰ هُدًى مِن رَبِّهِمْ **ط** وَأُولَٰئِكَ هُمُ الْمُفْلِحُونَ
- Surah Al-Baqarah 2:7: وَلَهُمْ عَذَابٌ عَظِيمٌ **ط**

---

### Symbol: ج (Waqf Ja'iz — وقف جائز)

rule_name: Waqf Ja'iz

#### Name: Permitted Stop (الوقف الجائز)
#### Symbol: ج (the letter jeem, from جائز)

#### Definition
Waqf Ja'iz means stopping is PERMITTED but not preferred — stopping and continuing are both equally acceptable. The meaning is somewhat complete but connecting to the next phrase is also fine.

#### How to Recite
- Both stopping and continuing are allowed
- Neither is strongly preferred over the other
- The reciter may choose based on breath or preference

#### Quran Examples
- Surah Al-Baqarah 2:22: الَّذِي جَعَلَ لَكُمُ الْأَرْضَ فِرَاشًا **ج** وَالسَّمَاءَ بِنَاءً
  → Stopping on فِرَاشًا is permitted; continuing to وَالسَّمَاءَ is also fine
- Surah Al-Baqarah 2:137: فَإِنْ آمَنُوا بِمِثْلِ مَا آمَنتُم بِهِ فَقَدِ اهْتَدَوا **ج** وَإِن تَوَلَّوْا
- Surah Al-Imran 3:4: وَأَنزَلَ الْفُرْقَانَ **ج** إِنَّ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا

---

### Symbol: لا (La Waqf — لا وقف)

rule_name: La Waqf

#### Name: Do Not Stop (لا وقف)
#### Symbol: لا (the word "laa" — "no")

#### Definition
La Waqf means stopping is NOT PERMITTED here because the meaning is incomplete — the current phrase is connected to what follows and stopping would either distort the meaning or make the sentence grammatically broken.

However: if the reciter MUST stop due to running out of breath, they may stop — but MUST return to the beginning of the phrase (or an appropriate earlier point) and continue. They cannot simply resume from after the لا symbol.

#### How to Recite
- Do NOT stop here if possible
- Continue the recitation to the next meaningful pause point
- If forced to stop: go back and resume from the beginning of the current phrase

#### Quran Examples
- Surah Al-Baqarah 2:2-3: ذَٰلِكَ الْكِتَابُ لَا رَيْبَ فِيهِ هُدًى لِّلْمُتَّقِينَ **لا** الَّذِينَ يُؤْمِنُونَ
  → Do not stop between المتقين and الذين — the next phrase describes them
- Surah Al-Fatiha 1:6-7: اهْدِنَا الصِّرَاطَ الْمُسْتَقِيمَ **لا** صِرَاطَ الَّذِينَ أَنْعَمْتَ عَلَيْهِمْ
  → The straight path is immediately defined in the next phrase — do not break them apart

---

### Symbol: ص (Waqf Murakhkhas — وقف مرخص)

rule_name: Waqf Murakhkhas

#### Name: Permitted Stop due to Length (الوقف المرخص) / Waqf Kafi
#### Symbol: ص (the letter sad, from مُرَخَّص)

#### Definition
Waqf Murakhkhas (also called Waqf Kafi — وقف كافي in some printings) means stopping is PERMITTED due to the length of the verse, even though it is not the ideal place to stop. It is a concession (rukhsa — رخصة) given to the reciter because the verse or sentence is very long and the reciter may need to breathe.

The meaning at this point is sufficiently complete for a pause, but the connection to the next phrase is strong.

#### How to Recite
- Stopping is acceptable as a concession
- Preferred to continue if the reciter can
- After stopping, resume from the next word (not from the beginning of the verse)

#### Quran Examples
- Surah Al-Baqarah 2:37: فَتَلَقَّىٰ آدَمُ مِن رَّبِّهِ كَلِمَاتٍ **ص** فَتَابَ عَلَيْهِ
  → Long context; stopping on كَلِمَاتٍ is acceptable
- Found frequently in long verses (Surah Al-Baqarah, Surah An-Nisa, Surah Al-Imran)

## Rules of Waqf on Tanween and Special Endings

### Waqf on Tanween Fath (ـً)
When stopping on a word ending with tanween fath (double fatha ـً), the tanween is replaced by an alif sound, and that alif is elongated 2 counts (Madd 'Iwad):
- رَحِيمًا (raheeman) → pause → رَحِيمَا (raheemaa)
- عَلِيمًا (aleeman) → pause → عَلِيمَا

Exception: If the word ends in ta marbuta (ـة) with tanween fath, stop with an "h" sound (the ta marbuta becomes ha):
- رَحْمَةً → pause → رَحْمَه

### Waqf on Ta Marbuta (ة)
When stopping on ta marbuta (ة), it is pronounced as ha (ه):
- الْجَنَّةُ (al-jannah) — when pausing: "al-jannah" (with h sound)
- رَحْمَةٌ (rahmatun) — when pausing: "rahmah"
- سُورَةٌ (sooratun) — when pausing: "soorah"

### Waqf on Tanween Kasra (ـٍ) and Tanween Damma (ـٌ)
When stopping, the tanween is dropped entirely:
- عَلِيمٌ (aleemun) → pause → عَلِيم (aleem)
- هُدًى (hudan — this has fath) → pause → هُدَى (hudaa) — alif added

## Types of Waqf by Category

| Arabic Term | Meaning | Effect on Recitation |
|-------------|---------|----------------------|
| وقف تام (Waqf Taam) | Perfect Stop | Meaning fully complete; no connection to next — best stop |
| وقف كافٍ (Waqf Kaafi) | Sufficient Stop | Meaning complete grammatically; slight connection ahead |
| وقف حسن (Waqf Hasan) | Good Stop | Meaning partially complete; resuming from here is good |
| وقف قبيح (Waqf Qabeeh) | Bad Stop | Meaning incomplete or distorted — must not stop here |

## Summary of Mushaf Symbols

| Symbol | Name | Meaning | Action |
|--------|------|---------|--------|
| مـ | Waqf Lazim | Compulsory stop | MUST stop |
| ط | Waqf Mutlaq | Absolute/preferred stop | Preferred to stop |
| ج | Waqf Ja'iz | Permitted stop | Stop or continue — equal |
| لا | La Waqf | Do not stop | Do NOT stop (or return) |
| ص | Waqf Murakhkhas | Concession stop | May stop if needed |
| قلى | Qeela alayhil waqf | Stopping preferred by some scholars | Stopping preferred |
| صلى | Wasl preferred | Continuing is preferred | Preferred to continue |
| ۚ | Waqf Aula | Stopping is better | Better to stop |
| ۛ — ۛ | Mu'anaqah (معانقة) | Connected pair stops — stop on one only | Stop on ONE of the two |
