# Qalqala Rules (أحكام القلقلة)

rule_name: Qalqala

## Definition
Qalqala (قلقلة) means "echoing," "vibration," or "disturbance." It refers to a strong, bouncing echo sound produced when reciting one of the five Qalqala letters with a sukoon (ـْ). The sound is similar to a brief, involuntary echo at the point of articulation.

The word Qalqala itself comes from the root ق-ل-ق-ل which conveys the meaning of shaking or rolling back and forth.

### The Five Qalqala Letters (حروف القلقلة)
ق — ط — ب — ج — د

Memory aid: قُطُبُ جَدّ (qutbu jadd — "polar ancestor") — or simply remember the word: قطبجد

All five letters are stop consonants (shidda — شدة) — letters at which the airflow is completely blocked at the point of articulation, then suddenly released. This complete blockage followed by release is what creates the qalqala echo.

### Why These Letters?
These five letters share two characteristics:
1. They are letters of shidda (شدة) — the airflow is completely stopped
2. They become difficult to pronounce with a clean sukoon — a natural echo results from trying to release the articulation point

## Types of Qalqala

### Type 1: Qalqala Sughra (قلقلة صغرى) — Minor Qalqala

rule_name: Qalqala Sughra

#### Definition
Qalqala Sughra (قلقلة صغرى) means "minor echoing." It occurs when a Qalqala letter has a sukoon in the MIDDLE of a word (i.e., the recitation continues after it — it is not a stopping point). The echo is present but lighter and shorter.

#### Condition
- Qalqala letter carries a sukoon (ـْ)
- It occurs in the middle of a word or the reciter continues to the next word without stopping
- The sukoon is not caused by pausing — it is an original sukoon in the text

#### How to Recite
- Pronounce the Qalqala letter with a short, light echo/bounce
- The echo is brief — just enough to release the articulation point clearly
- Do not add an extra vowel sound; the echo is a natural release

#### Quran Examples
- يَقْطَعُونَ (yaqta'oona) — Surah Al-Baqarah 2:27 — qaf with sukoon in middle of word
- اقْتَرَبَ (iqtaraba) — Surah Al-Anbiya 21:1 — qaf with sukoon at start, recitation continues
- وَيَبْسُطُ (wa yabsutu) — Surah Al-Baqarah 2:245 — ba with sukoon in middle
- وَالْفَجْرِ (wal-fajri) — Surah Al-Fajr 89:1 — jeem with sukoon, recitation continues
- خَلَقْنَاكُمْ (khalaqnaakum) — Surah Al-A'raf 7:11 — qaf with sukoon, continues
- أَطْعَمَهُم (at'amahum) — Surah Al-Quraysh 106:4 — ta with sukoon, continues
- يَجْعَلُونَ (yaj'aloona) — jeem with sukoon in middle
- أَبْتَغِي (abtaghee) — ba with sukoon in middle

### Type 2: Qalqala Kubra (قلقلة كبرى) — Major Qalqala

rule_name: Qalqala Kubra

#### Definition
Qalqala Kubra (قلقلة كبرى) means "major echoing." It occurs when a Qalqala letter is at the END of a word and the reciter STOPS (pauses/waqf) on it. The sukoon in this case is caused by the pause. The echo is larger, stronger, and more pronounced than Qalqala Sughra.

#### Condition
- Qalqala letter is at the end of a word
- The reciter stops (makes waqf) on that letter
- The letter receives a sukoon due to the pause

#### How to Recite
- Pronounce the Qalqala letter with a clear, strong, and audible echo
- The echo should be distinct and noticeable — roughly half a vowel sound in length
- The sound bounces off the articulation point: for qaf (ق) it is deep in the throat; for ta (ط) it is the tongue tip; for ba (ب) it is the lips
- The Qalqala Kubra is particularly emphatic when the letter has a shaddah (ـّ) — this is called Qalqala Akbar (قلقلة أكبر) by some scholars

#### Quran Examples
- قُلْ أَعُوذُ بِرَبِّ الْفَلَقِ (qul a'oodhu bi-rabbi l-falaq) — Surah Al-Falaq 113:1
  → Pausing on الْفَلَقِ: qaf at the end with Qalqala Kubra
- وَمِن شَرِّ غَاسِقٍ إِذَا وَقَبَ — Surah Al-Falaq 113:3
  → Pausing on وَقَبَ: ba at the end with Qalqala Kubra
- قُلْ هُوَ اللهُ أَحَدٌ — Surah Al-Ikhlas 112:1
  → Pausing on أَحَدٌ (احد): dal at the end — Qalqala Kubra
- اللهُ الصَّمَدُ — Surah Al-Ikhlas 112:2
  → Pausing on الصَّمَدُ: dal with shaddah context — strong Qalqala Kubra
- وَلَمْ يَكُن لَهُ كُفُوًا أَحَدٌ — Surah Al-Ikhlas 112:4
  → Pausing on أَحَدٌ: dal — Qalqala Kubra
- مِن شَرِّ مَا خَلَقَ — Surah Al-Falaq 113:2
  → Pausing on خَلَقَ: qaf — Qalqala Kubra
- وَالطَّارِقِ — Surah At-Tariq 86:1
  → Pausing on وَالطَّارِقِ: qaf — Qalqala Kubra
- إِنَّهُ عَلَى رَجْعِهِ لَقَادِرٌ — Surah At-Tariq 86:8
  → Pausing on لَقَادِرٌ: ra is not qalqala, but قَادِرٌ — dal — Qalqala Kubra

## Articulation Points of Qalqala Letters

| Letter | Name | Articulation Point | Echo Character |
|--------|------|--------------------|----------------|
| ق | Qaf | Deep back of tongue against soft palate (velum) | Deep, throaty echo |
| ط | Ta | Tip/blade of tongue against upper gum ridge | Strong, clear echo |
| ب | Ba | Both lips pressed together | Labial pop/echo |
| ج | Jeem | Middle of tongue against hard palate | Mid-palate echo |
| د | Dal | Tip of tongue against upper gum ridge (less emphatic than ط) | Light, front echo |

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
1. **Adding a full vowel**: The echo should NOT sound like a full fatha, kasra, or damma — it is just a natural release
2. **Omitting qalqala entirely**: Especially in Qalqala Sughra, beginners may suppress the echo — it must always be present
3. **Weak Qalqala Kubra**: When stopping, the echo must be clear and audible — a common error is making it too faint
4. **Applying qalqala to non-qalqala letters**: Only the five letters ق ط ب ج د — not to ك or ز or others
